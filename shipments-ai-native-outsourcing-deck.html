The operations gap
Why this matters

Freight forwarders do not lose margin on one task. They lose it across every handoff.

After a shipment is confirmed, the work fragments across customer emails, carrier portals, PDFs, spreadsheets, TMS screens, customer follow-ups, and manual checklists.

Cutoffs move faster than inboxes

SI, VGM, gate-in, documentation, and customs cutoffs are scattered across carrier confirmations and follow-up messages.

Operators re-key the same facts

Ports, parties, container details, commodity data, weights, package counts, references, and contact details are copied into multiple systems.

Exceptions are invisible until late

Missing invoices, BL mismatches, unanswered approvals, and carrier portal failures usually surface only when the shipment is already at risk.

Confirmation-to-sail work is high-volume, deadline-driven, and detail-heavy02

Implementation plan
Rollout

A practical pilot path that does not require a big-bang transformation.

1. Map the lane

Collect sample confirmations, SOPs, portal steps, TMS process, document checklist, and escalation rules.

2. Configure the service

Set fields, cut-off rules, customer email templates, carrier actions, credentials, and Fracto review queues.

3. Shadow operations

Run in parallel first: observe, assist, then take over agreed steps after the branch is comfortable.

4. Go live and scale

Move selected shipment volume to the managed pod, measure outcomes, and add carriers and branches iteratively.
