The hidden cost

Freight forwarders do not lose margin on one task. They lose it across every handoff.

Once a client confirms that a shipment should be booked, the work becomes a deadline-driven trail across inboxes, TMS screens, carrier portals, documents, customer approvals, and operator memory.

BCG notes that forwarding still carries an unusually high number of manual processes, including email, handoffs, and document exchange.
